Harmony and Creativity

The Sound of Our School

Explore the Artist

Our Music and Dance curriculum at Ikigai offers students the chance to explore their creative potential while developing technical skills and artistic expression. From the basics of rhythm and melody to advanced choreography and performance, students are guided through a structured yet inspiring journey.

Music Curriculum Overview

Elementary School

Introduction to Music

Students are introduced to basic music theory, rhythm, and melody through engaging activities, including singing, clapping, and introductory instruments like the recorder and percussion.

Music Appreciation

Exposure to a wide range of musical styles and cultures, teaching students how to listen critically and appreciate diverse forms of music.

Introduction to Choir

Group singing and learning basic vocal techniques.

Middle School

Foundational Dance Techniques

Building strength, flexibility, and coordination through ballet, jazz, contemporary, and hip-hop dance styles.

Choreography Basics

Introduction to creating short routines and understanding the relationship between music and movement.

Dance History and Appreciation

Exposure to different dance traditions and cultures, developing a deeper understanding of the art form.

High School

Dance Theory and Criticism

Understanding the theoretical elements of dance, including anatomy, kinesiology, and the critique of professional dance performances.

Choreography and Composition

Students learn to create, refine, and present their own original dance pieces, emphasizing storytelling through movement.

Performance Opportunities

Participation in school productions, recitals, and competitive dance events.
